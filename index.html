Who are we?

Get Your Indie Fix are a group of enthusiastic indie music-lovers who are keen to spread the word about the newest and coolest artists that are breaking ground on the indie scene.

In the Spotlight: English Teacher

Read Time: 5 mins

English Teacher are Lily Fontaine, Lewis Whitling, Douglas Frost and Nicholas Eden. Hailing from Leeds, the band formed in 2020 after meeting at Leeds Conservatoire. April 2024 saw the release of their debut album 'This Could be Texas' and they were subsequently awarded the Mercury Prize in September, fending off bookies favourites' Charli XCX and the Last Dinner Party.

If you're not familiar with the works of English Teacher, you might be inclined to take a listen if you are a fan of Black Country, New Road or Dry Cleaning. Lily Fontaine tends to use the sprechgesang style favoured by these bands, but it could be their northern charm that sets English Teacher apart from their predecessors.

'We just thought we'd make a band' - Lily Fontaine, as she accepts the Mercury Prize, 2024

Looking for where to start? Punchy anthems 'The World's Biggest Paving Slab' and 'Nearly Daffodils' will certainly grab your attention, but softer, more tender 'Albatross' and 'This Could be Texas' exhibit the truly magnificent range that Lily Fontaine can master.
